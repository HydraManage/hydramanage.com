Why a Great Chatter Changes Everything

​

In my experience, a skilled chatter can multiply your revenue by as much as 15x to 20x compared to subscriptions alone.

Even a mid-level chatter can bring in daily sales that far exceed the base subscriber fees.

The difference between “just okay” and “amazing” chatters is huge—and here’s why it matters:

Motivated Creators: When models see higher earnings from premium sales, they’re more willing to create content and promote themselves.

Referrals: Creators will happily recommend you to their friends if you consistently help them earn real money.

Loyalty: A strong backend (chatting and sales) helps keep your models from jumping ship to other agencies that might promise bigger payouts.

The Tough Part: Finding & Training Chatters​

So, if chatting is so important, how come every agency doesn’t just hire an all-star team?

Because it’s tricky to identify and recruit the right people for this role.

Here are a few hurdles:

Location vs. Talent: Many agencies hire from lower-cost countries. While it saves money, the pool of true sales rockstars there can be limited.

Cultural or Language Barriers: Even good English speakers might stumble when pretending to be an American or British creator.

Sales Is a Learned Skill: Most people have never sold anything in their lives. Teaching them from zero requires a robust training system.

A big mistake I see is agency owners scrambling to find a good chatter by quickly testing a bunch of random people. This can:

Lead to accidental TOS violations on OnlyFans (resulting in bans).

Waste time cycling through unqualified hires.

Leave you without a repeatable way to train future staff.

Solution: Develop an in-house system or partner with a proven third party.

If you can hire a chatter who’s not only great at selling but also knows how to recruit and train others, you’ll be sitting on gold.

Management: The Umbrella That Shields It All

​

If your agency only had to handle three things—recruiting creators, driving traffic, and selling content—it would already be tough.

But there’s another layer that weaves these parts together: management.

Staying on top of creators’ moods, daily tasks, and personal drama is no joke.

It’s easy to slip into a cycle of chasing them to film new videos, dealing with flakiness, or smoothing over misunderstandings.

Why do so many agency owners get cynical about models?

Because a lot of creators do come across as unmotivated or unreliable.

To be fair, some are fantastic—ambitious, punctual, open to advice.

But many get into OnlyFans hoping it’s effortless money. When they find out there’s actual work involved, they freak out or bail.

Your job as a manager is to filter out the bad apples early and keep the good ones engaged.

​

Biggest Creator Hurdles: Insecurities and Laziness​

Some creators might be shy or fear getting made fun of online.

Others simply don’t want to put in the consistent effort that success demands.

Either way, management means:

Offering them daily motivation and content ideas.

Setting a clear path forward with easy, measurable goals.

Giving feedback gently (yet firmly) so they grow without feeling crushed.

​

​

Five Steps to Manage Creators Like a Pro

​

1. Start with a Clear Onboarding

When you bring a new model on, show them exactly how your agency works.

Explain the tools you’ll use, what you expect from them, and how you’ll communicate.

A lot of confusion can be avoided if you outline everything right away.

2. Map Out a Success Plan

Most creators have no clue what real success looks like.

Make a simple roadmap: “First we get your social pages set up, then we focus on daily content, then we grow subscribers. After that, we add a chatter to maximize your revenue.”

3. Daily Inspiration

Creators often run out of ideas for photos or short-form videos.

Feed them daily examples of what’s working online—short clips, trending audios, or fun themes.

Let them copy proven concepts at first instead of getting stuck trying to “invent” viral content.

4. Feedback Sandwich

When you see a lackluster video, don’t immediately slam them.

Start with a compliment on something they did right.

Then give one or two small improvements they could make.

End with an optimistic note so they stay motivated.

5. Set Big and Small Goals

Large goals (like earning a certain amount or reaching a big subscriber milestone) keep them inspired.

Smaller goals (like hitting a certain daily content number or snagging a few new subs each day) give them quick wins.

​​​​​​

Show Them the Path Before They Balk

Creators fail for lots of reasons—mostly because they realize it’s more work than they thought.

By keeping them focused on daily tasks and celebrating small wins, you hold their hand through the tough early phase.

Once their view counts rise and they see real paychecks, they become self-motivated.
